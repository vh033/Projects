Ages 0 to 5

Born into a family with an older brother and older sister, no wonder I starve for attention now....

Look at how cute I am, my first little viola. Little did I know how much pain it would cause me... The cat is Phoebe, who lived for another 18 years after that. That's the family home that we have had all the time.

Key Points or Memories

  • Started playing viola
  • Loved walking to kindy (kindergarten) with my dad. I had a sweater with a hood, and he would put my apple for snack time in the hood hanging behind me
  • Started playing the piano
  • Started at Westburn primary school, the same as my brother and sister

Ages 6 to 10

During this time I had started going to school, presumably could talk now and even started trumpet

Key Points or Memories

  • Started to play trumpet at 8 years old
  • Must have started to play sports: soccer and teeball and perhaps tennis
  • Got glasses
  • First crush, a girl called Gabby
Take notice of my shirt, it says "What" as in "What Now". "What Now" was a kids tv show on Saturday mornings with games and other funny stuff like that. Somewhat informative for kids and somewhat just fun entertainment to keep us out of Mum and Dad's hair...

My first pair of horrible glasses. I guess maybe we thought they would be temporary, but sadly....I had this pair until university

Aren't I so cool. No idea why I'm biting my lip so seductively as well.

Ages 11 to 15

Hard to remember what happened here.

But, there are a few things. I moved from Westburn primary school, the uniform that you see in the previous photo to Breens Intermediate school. This was quite a big shift as the demographic was really different, the music department was much worse and generally education was lower. However it gave me a good chance to develop social skills of more "common" people, the type who would become plumbers and electricians.

In fact one of my friends from this time became a plumber or electrician and did work on Mum and Dad's house.

Key Points or Memories

  • Changed schools to Breens Intermediate. Tricky to leave friends, but the school was right down the street
  • Masturbated for the first time
  • Started high school
  • First cell-phone, literally a phone. Couldn't even text
  • Started playing basketball
  • Stopped playing basketball and joined the school orchestra
  • Discovered jazz, and joined jazz ensembles at school
  • Family trip to Europe: first time overseas to somewhere other than Australia
I think this photo is roughly at this time, around 12 years old, at least it's the right room that I would have practiced in. I use to love the animal curtains in the background, at a time like right now when I can't sleep, I used to just look at all the shapes of them.

You can also see the basketball shoes in the foreground, left. And the viola shoulder rest I'm using is the same one I'm using now!

Ages 16 to 20

The dreaded proper teenage years. Luckily I wasn't too much of a horrible teenager I think...

All dressed up in the Burnside High School Uniform. It must have been a Showcase performance or prize giving or something like that. I might have been actually more like 15 here?

Key Points or Memories

  • Got a new viola and later a new trumpet
  • Started to really really enjoy music, especially at 17/18
  • Conducted for the first time
  • Scholarship trip to Berlin for specific German Class students
  • Studied one year at University of Canterbury. Some music papers, but mostly german and russian language, and related artsy papers
  • First paid gig as a musician, Sweeney Todd
  • Began music studies at Victoria University/New Zealand School of Music
Enjoying my new trumpet, it has special valve things that you can use to change the sound which is cool, plus I loved the pretty engraving on the bell. It's the trumpet I still have, now in Auckland
